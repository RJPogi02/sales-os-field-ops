## Guardrails checked

- Conversation Flow is deterministic guidance; it does not call an external model or present a suggestion as completed work.
- The Command Center reads operational data and provides a labeled rule-based recommendation; it cannot edit pricing, statuses, assignments, or CRM records.
- Final pricing remains owned by the human approver configured in Company Profile.
- Default company facts contain no 100-truck or Batangas-quarry claim.
- Local mode does not invent a connected team or leaderboard.
- CSV/XLSX import does not mutate the CRM until the operator confirms the reviewed mapping and scope.
- Incomplete imports enter Research rather than the callable daily queue.
- Private/local import is the default; team scope is explicit.
- Legacy pricing-stage and approver-note names are migrated for compatibility.

## Exact pre-use checklist

1. Export the current CRM CSV before testing imports or Team Hub migrations.
2. Open **Settings → Company** and confirm the company name, operator, fleet, quarry/source locations, materials, credentials, and final pricing approver are accurate.
3. Open one cold lead, one contacted lead, and one quotation lead. Confirm Conversation Flow changes and every suggested claim is supported by Company Profile.
4. Open **Command**, switch 7D/30D, and compare the calls, funnel, pipeline, objections, and territory counts with Reports/CRM.
5. Import a disposable CSV with one valid row, one duplicate, and one missing-phone row. Verify preview/mapping, Research routing, and summary before deleting the disposable rows.
6. Import a disposable XLSX with renamed headers and verify the mapper does not commit until confirmation.
7. Test the same import once as private and, only in a non-production Team Hub, once as team-visible.
8. Confirm Call Mode, quick results, profile send pack, quote-ready validation, human pricing handoff, CRM editing, CSV export, and daily report still behave normally.
9. Test Light, Dark, Liquid Glass, Aurora Glass Ops, Rose Quartz, and Velvet Orchid in both workspace and Call Mode; enable reduced motion and confirm ambient effects stop or simplify.
10. If Team Hub will be deployed, apply `supabase/schema.sql`, `supabase/migrations/20260714_team_call_claim_reliability.sql`, and `supabase/migrations/20260715_v009_team_roles.sql` in a non-production project.
11. In that project, test owner, manager, representative, pending member, approval-required join, assignment, manager gating, private lead visibility, and a simultaneous claim from two laptops.
12. Export the CRM again and compare row count, status, phone, source, ownership, and pricing fields before using the migrated build for real calls.

## Remaining limits

- Google Places and Supabase still need live project-level validation; neither service is preconfigured or bundled with credentials.
- The device PIN is a local casual-access gate, not localStorage encryption or account recovery.
- Manager recommendations are rule-based until a later, explicitly approved AI phase is designed.
- Team Hub roles and row-level-security changes should be reviewed against the company's final authority model before production rollout.
- XLSX support intentionally reads data rows only; it does not preserve formulas, formatting, macros, or workbook behavior.
- The source ZIP requires Node.js LTS and installs dependencies on first launch.
- Main-bundle code splitting remains a future performance task.
